Core Question

You just got access to the most powerful information tools ever created. What do you need to know — today — to use them without putting yourself, your patients, or your organization at risk?

This isn't a compliance manual or a cybersecurity course. It's a practical orientation for people who are excited about AI and want to use it responsibly. If you're a clinician, a medical assistant, a front desk coordinator, a billing specialist, or anyone who handles sensitive information — this is for you.

The Ground Rule

AI tools are extraordinarily capable. That capability comes with real risk — not theoretical risk, but the kind that leads to breached patient data, compromised credentials, and regulatory fines measured in millions. The good news: most of these risks are preventable with basic practices.

Not Fear — Awareness
Understanding risks lets you use AI confidently, not avoid it entirely
Practical, Not Perfect
We focus on the 20% of practices that prevent 80% of problems
Everyone's Responsibility
Security isn't just IT's job — every person who touches a tool is a link in the chain

What We'll Cover

This guide has five focused modules after this introduction:

  • Shadow AI — The tools your team is already using without your knowledge, and why that matters.
  • Digital Hygiene — Passwords, disk encryption, and the basic practices that everything else depends on.
  • HIPAA, BAAs, and AI — What the law actually requires, which platforms comply, and why consumer vs. enterprise is the distinction that will save your career.
  • Your AI Tools — Agents, developer tools, browser extensions, and the nine categories of tools that catch even careful people off guard.
  • The Checklist — A printable reference you can use before adopting any new AI tool.

The whole guide takes about 20 minutes. You can read it straight through or jump to the topics most relevant to your work.
