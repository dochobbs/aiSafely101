HIPAA, BAAs, and AI

What HIPAA actually requires, which platforms offer BAAs, and why the consumer vs. enterprise distinction will save your career.

HIPAA was written in 1996 for paper charts and fax machines. AI breaks almost every assumption it was built on.

The BAA Requirement

A Business Associate Agreement (BAA) is a legal contract where an AI vendor accepts liability for safeguarding protected health information (PHI). Without one, using the tool with PHI is a HIPAA violation. Period.

A BAA must cover three things: Permitted Uses (the vendor cannot use patient data to train their public models), Breach Notification (the vendor must notify you within a specific timeframe if a breach occurs), and Subcontractors (the vendor's downstream partners must also be compliant).

Consumer vs. Enterprise — The Critical Distinction

ChatGPT (the free or Plus version) does NOT have a BAA. ChatGPT Enterprise does. Claude.ai (consumer) does NOT have a BAA. The Claude API for commercial customers does.

Using the consumer version of any AI tool with patient data is a HIPAA violation, even if the enterprise version of that same product is compliant.

The pattern: API and enterprise tiers offer BAAs. Consumer tiers do not. The consumer version of Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini on your phone is NOT the same product as the enterprise version your hospital might have approved.

Foundation Models vs. Cloud Providers

There are two ways to get a BAA for AI: go directly to the company that made the model (OpenAI, Anthropic), or go through a cloud provider that hosts it (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud). For most small practices and clinics, the cloud provider route is easier — and often cheaper.

Here's why: AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud already have healthcare compliance built into their standard agreements. If your practice already uses Google Workspace, you might be three clicks away from a BAA (see below). These platforms host the same models — Claude runs on AWS Bedrock, GPT-4 runs on Azure, Gemini runs on Google Cloud — with the compliance infrastructure already wrapped around them.

Google Workspace Makes This Easy

If your organization already uses Google Workspace (Gmail, Drive, Calendar), you may be closer to a BAA than you think. Google's BAA is available through the Admin Console with electronic acceptance — no sales calls, no custom contracts, no lawyers required. It's one of the fastest paths to HIPAA-compliant AI for small practices.

How to Actually Sign a BAA

The process varies by provider, but here's what you can expect. We've included direct links where possible.

Google Workspace (Easiest Path)

  1. Log in to your Google Admin Console as a Super Administrator
  2. Navigate to Account → Legal and Compliance
  3. Find the Google Workspace HIPAA Business Associate Amendment
  4. Click Review and Accept
  5. Confirm whether you're a Covered Entity or Business Associate
  6. Done — the electronic acceptance is legally binding. Take a screenshot for your records.

After signing, review Google's HIPAA Included Functionality list to confirm which services are covered. Note: NotebookLM is NOT covered under the Workspace BAA.

AWS Bedrock (Self-Service)

  1. Log in to the AWS Management Console
  2. Navigate to AWS Artifact
  3. Accept the AWS Business Associate Addendum
  4. Amazon Bedrock is automatically included under the AWS BAA — no separate step needed

This covers Claude (via Bedrock), Amazon Titan, and other models on the platform. See AWS HIPAA Eligible Services for the full list.

OpenAI API

  1. Email baa@openai.com with your organization details and intended use case
  2. OpenAI typically responds within 1–2 business days
  3. Approval is case-by-case; confirm your endpoints are zero-retention eligible

For ChatGPT Enterprise or ChatGPT for Healthcare, work with OpenAI Sales. Note: ChatGPT Business is not eligible for a BAA.

Anthropic (Claude API / Enterprise)

  1. Contact Anthropic Sales to discuss your deployment
  2. BAAs require Zero Data Retention (ZDR) to be enabled
  3. For Enterprise plans, the HIPAA-ready offering is sales-assisted only

Review Anthropic's BAA documentation for the full list of what's covered and what's not. Important: Claude Code is only covered under the BAA when ZDR is enabled.

Azure OpenAI

If you have an existing Azure subscription, the BAA is typically included in your enterprise agreement. Contact your Microsoft account representative or visit the Azure HIPAA compliance page. You can download the BAA directly from the Service Trust Portal.

January 2025 HIPAA Security Rule Update

The HHS Office for Civil Rights proposed the first major HIPAA Security Rule update in 20 years. Key changes: the distinction between "required" and "addressable" safeguards is removed, and stricter expectations for risk management, encryption, and resilience are introduced. Organizations deploying AI should treat these as mandatory.

AI Scribes That Sign BAAs

Ambient AI scribes are among the most adopted AI tools in healthcare. Major providers with HIPAA-compliant solutions:

  • Nuance DAX Copilot — HITRUST CSF and SOC 2 Type II certified. Integrated with Epic and other major EHRs.
  • Abridge — BAA available. Used by major health systems including UPMC and UCI Health.
  • Ambience Healthcare — BAA available. Enterprise-focused with deep EHR integration.
  • Freed AI — HIPAA and HITECH compliant. Zero storage of patient recordings. Popular with solo/small practices (~$99/mo).
  • DeepScribe — BAA available. Focused on specialty practices.
  • Twofold Health — HIPAA/HITECH compliant. Recordings never stored. Flat pricing ($49/mo).
The Scribe Evaluation Checklist

Verify four things: (1) a signed BAA, (2) encrypted data transmission and storage, (3) access controls and audit logs, (4) clear data retention and deletion policies. If they can't document all four, walk away.

The Bottom Line

If an AI tool touches patient data and doesn't have a BAA, stop using it for that purpose today. Not next quarter, not after the compliance review — today. Everything else in this module is nuance. That one rule isn't.
